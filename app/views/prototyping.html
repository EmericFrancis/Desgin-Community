Prototyping

We need prototypes to share and test designs out before creating the actual service. The GOV.UK service manual explains why we make prototypes. It is also important to ensure we keep a history of why designs are iterated upon.

  • Low fidelity prototypes

    These are quick, cheap and easy! Create paper prototypes to quickly share and communicate ideas with other team members and designers. There are some GOV.UK templates that can be printed and used.

  • Medium fidelity prototypes

    Create these quickly in Axure, or Sketch and InVision and can be easily amended. We mainly use Axure and have a widget library to get the correct look and feel.

  • High fidelity prototypes

    There is a GOV.UK prototype kit that is used to create coded prototypes which give a more realistic interactive experience for the user. If you've not used the kit before, there is a tutorial that can be completed to help you get started once you've got the kit installed.
